# FSA Substantiation Rules

Use these rules as a local workflow checklist, not as tax, legal, medical, payroll, or plan-design advice. Apply the user's plan document first when supplied.

## Evidence Basis

- IRS Publication 969 explains Health FSAs, HRAs, HSAs, qualified medical expenses, coverage-period concepts, and written third-party substantiation for FSA reimbursements: https://www.irs.gov/publications/p969
- IRS Publication 502 is the baseline reference for medical and dental expense categories: https://www.irs.gov/publications/p502
- IRS cafeteria-plan guidance warns that mistakenly reimbursing unsubstantiated medical expenses can undermine tax exclusion treatment: https://www.irs.gov/irb/2017-47_IRB
- FSAFEDS claim guidance requires appropriate supporting documentation and recommends EOBs for health care receipts when insurance paid part of the expense: https://www.fsafeds.gov/file
- FSAFEDS supporting documentation examples list EOBs, itemized provider receipts, LMNs, and dependent-care claim support: https://www.fsafeds.gov/support/resources/claims
- FSAFEDS eligible expense pages remind participants to save itemized receipts and supporting documentation because eligibility may need verification: https://www.fsafeds.gov/explore/hcfsa/expenses
- FSAFEDS Limited Expense Health Care FSA material limits the account to dental/vision and related LPFSA rules unless plan terms allow broader post-deductible use: https://www.fsafeds.gov/explore/lex-hcfsa
- Optum dependent-care claim guidance highlights required fields such as provider, dependent/person receiving care, service description, date, amount, and provider SSN/TIN/signature for third-party receipts: https://www.optum.com/en/library/health-finances/page.claims-resource-center.get-your-dcfsa-claim-approved.html

## Packet Requirements

For every claim packet, verify:

- Account type: Health FSA, LPFSA, DCFSA, HRA, or HSA.
- Date basis: service date or care date, not only payment date.
- Coverage period: service date falls within account coverage and plan runout rules.
- Amount: amount requested is visible and matches EOB/receipt support.
- Recipient: patient or dependent is identified in a way the plan can match.
- Provider: provider/vendor/caregiver name is visible.
- Service description: receipt explains what was provided, not only that payment occurred.
- Duplicate status: claim was not already reimbursed by the same account, another account, insurance, or employer reimbursement.
- Portal boundary: live portal submission or repayment stays with an authorized human owner.

## Health FSA, HRA, And HSA Checks

- Prefer EOBs when insurance processed the service.
- A card receipt or bank statement alone is payment proof, not an itemized receipt.
- Receipts should show provider, patient, service date, description, and amount.
- OTC, wellness, therapy, compounded medication, equipment, and nonstandard categories may need plan-specific evidence, prescription, or LMN support.
- LMNs should be current, match the claimed item/service, and not be invented by the agent.
- HSA checks are recordkeeping support only; the account holder remains responsible for qualified medical expense determinations.

## LPFSA Checks

- Default LPFSA packets should be dental/vision unless the user's plan document says post-deductible medical expenses are allowed.
- If post-deductible medical reimbursement is claimed, request deductible-met evidence or benefits-owner review.
- Do not infer HSA eligibility or HDHP status.

## Dependent Care FSA Checks

- Verify dependent name, provider name, care dates, service description, and amount.
- If provider certification is used instead of an itemized receipt, check signature/date and provider TIN/SSN requirements when the plan form asks for them.
- Confirm the packet states care was work-related or otherwise matches the user's plan instructions.
- Flag prepaid future care. Many plans reimburse care after it has been provided, even if payment was made earlier.
- Do not give dependent-care tax-credit optimization advice unless the user asks and provides a tax-professional-reviewed policy context.

## Severity Guide

- `blocker`: claim is likely to be rejected, offset, clawed back, or unsafe to submit until repaired.
- `review`: claim may be valid but needs plan-owner or provider clarification.
- `info`: keep evidence or highlight the field for easier review.
