# Unemployment Appeal Rules

Use these rules as workflow checks, not as legal conclusions.

## Core Evidence Gates

- Determination gate: identify the exact determination or decision being appealed, the issue label, the mailing or issue date, and the appeal deadline.
- Hearing notice gate: capture hearing date, call-in or login instructions, parties, issue list, exhibit rules, evidence submission method, and accessibility or language needs.
- Issue mapping gate: map each issue to the records and witnesses that prove or rebut the facts. Avoid broad life-story narratives when the hearing issue is narrower.
- Evidence exchange gate: exhibits usually need to reach the hearing office and every listed party before the hearing. Track delivery method, date, and proof.
- Witness gate: firsthand witnesses are stronger than secondhand summaries. Record name, role, topic, contact, availability, and whether the party can question them.
- Continuity gate: claimants who remain unemployed commonly must keep weekly certifications, work-search records, and contact/address information current during appeal.
- Live action gate: filing, uploading, withdrawing, reopening, requesting subpoenas, joining hearings, or contacting an agency is live owner action.

## Issue-Specific Checks

### Misconduct Or Discharge

Look for:

- The final incident or policy violation alleged.
- Employer policy, handbook, or rule.
- Proof the claimant knew or should have known the rule.
- Warning, writeup, investigation, video, time record, message, or direct witness evidence.
- Claimant statement that addresses the allegation without contradicting prior statements.

Do not decide whether misconduct occurred. Report whether the packet can identify the allegation, evidence, firsthand testimony, and disputed facts.

### Voluntary Quit Or Good Cause

Look for:

- Resignation date and reason.
- Timeline of attempts to preserve employment when relevant.
- Working-condition, health, caregiver, schedule, pay, safety, transportation, relocation, or domestic-violence records when user says those are the reason.
- Communications with employer, HR, manager, union, doctor, school, caregiver, or agency.

Do not conclude the reason is legally good cause. Report whether the story is tied to records and dates.

### Able, Available, Work Search, Or Refusal

Look for:

- Weekly certifications and work-search logs.
- Availability restrictions and date ranges.
- Medical, caregiver, school, transportation, or scheduling records if they explain availability.
- Job offer, referral, refusal, interview, or start-date records.

### Overpayment Or Retroactive Employer Appeal

Look for:

- Overpayment notice, amount, weeks, reason, and appeal/waiver deadlines.
- Payment history and certifications for the affected weeks.
- Employer appeal notice or decision that changed eligibility.
- Records showing claimant reliance, repayment plan, waiver request, or agency error if user asks about those topics.

## Common Failure Modes

- Appeal filed after the deadline with no late-filing explanation or proof.
- User has a hearing date but no hearing packet or issue list.
- Evidence was gathered but not exchanged to all parties.
- Exhibits are unnumbered or mixed with irrelevant documents.
- Claimant stops weekly certifications during appeal.
- Misconduct response addresses general unfairness but not the final incident, policy, knowledge, or witness evidence.
- Quit response lacks a dated reason, preservation attempts, or supporting records.
- User asks the agent to upload, file, withdraw, or contact the agency directly.

## Report Language

Use:

- "This packet is missing..."
- "The hearing issue appears to require..."
- "Ask the authorized owner, agency, representative, or counsel to verify..."
- "Do not upload until the owner confirms..."

Avoid:

- "You are eligible."
- "You will win."
- "The employer committed fraud."
- "The agency must accept this."
- "I filed/submitted/contacted..."
