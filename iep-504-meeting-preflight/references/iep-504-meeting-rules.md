# IEP/504 Meeting Rules

Use this reference for packet-readiness classification. Do not use it to make legal, eligibility, placement, or clinical conclusions.

## Evidence Gates

- **Prior plan**: The packet should identify the current IEP, 504 plan, or absence of a plan. A meeting about changing services or accommodations is weak without the prior plan language.
- **Current evaluation**: Requested eligibility, service, placement, assistive-technology, or support changes should connect to current evaluation, reevaluation, observation, work-sample, or progress evidence. Treat evaluations older than three years as stale for review unless the user supplies a current reevaluation or district-specific context.
- **Progress data**: Annual review, service-change, and goal-change packets should include present levels, baseline data, goal progress, and how progress is measured.
- **Implementation evidence**: Concerns that services or accommodations were not delivered need service logs, accommodation logs, provider notes, class evidence, or communication history.
- **Parent or adult-student concerns**: The packet should include a concise concern statement and requested discussion topics before the meeting.
- **Notice and consent**: Evaluation, reevaluation, records, and service-change issues should identify consent status, prior written notice, meeting notice, or records-request status when applicable.
- **Behavior or discipline**: Manifestation, suspension, safety, or behavior-support concerns should include discipline notice, incident dates, behavior data, behavior plan, and support history.
- **Transition**: Transition planning concerns should include age/grade context, transition goals, postsecondary interests, agency or course-planning evidence, and progress data.
- **Assistive technology**: Device, software, access, or communication-support requests should include trial data, access barriers, prior tools, usage notes, and evaluation or specialist input.

## Decision Rules

- **Hold meeting packet pending evidence repair** when a missing record would likely make the meeting vague, unsupported, or impossible to verify locally.
- **Review before meeting** when no hard blocker is present but owner questions, timelines, or evidence labels need cleanup.
- **Packet appears ready for authorized owner review** only when the packet includes the plan, evidence, concerns, dates, and meeting questions needed for an authorized person to review.

## Common Failure Modes

- Asking for a service increase without baseline data, goal progress, or a service log.
- Bringing only anecdotes when the issue is implementation; logs and dated examples are needed.
- Treating a 504 accommodation problem like an IEP goal problem, or the reverse.
- Missing the meeting notice, prior written notice, evaluation consent, or records-request trail.
- Using raw medical or therapy records when a redacted summary or school-relevant evidence is enough.
- Asking the agent to accuse the school, file a complaint, or send a live portal/email action.

## Safety Language

Use neutral phrasing:

- "The packet lacks current evaluation evidence for this requested change."
- "Ask the authorized owner to verify the district deadline and notice requirements."
- "Prepare a question for the team about how progress will be measured."

Avoid:

- "The school violated the law."
- "The student is entitled to this placement."
- "This diagnosis proves eligibility."
- "Submit this complaint now."
