# FMLA Certification Rules

Use this reference to guide readiness checks. Keep conclusions administrative and evidence-based; do not decide legal entitlement, approval, denial, discipline, retaliation, accommodation, or litigation strategy.

## Core Timing Rules To Check

- Medical certification is commonly due at least 15 calendar days after the employer requests it, unless it is not practicable despite diligent, good-faith efforts.
- If an employer finds a certification incomplete or insufficient, the defect should be identified in writing and the employee generally receives at least seven calendar days to cure.
- Treat missing due dates, missing request dates, past-due certification, past-due cure, and undocumented extension requests as deadline risks that need authorized owner review.
- Do not use the script to submit forms, request extensions, or contact employers/providers.

## Completeness Signals

Check whether the packet contains:

- Eligibility notice and rights/responsibilities notice when the employer has triggered FMLA handling.
- Certification request date, due date, submitted date, and delivery proof.
- Provider signature/date and provider contact information.
- Serious-health-condition selection or equivalent explanation on the form.
- Incapacity duration, treatment schedule, reduced schedule or intermittent frequency/duration when applicable.
- Statement tying the requested leave to inability to work or need to care for a covered family member.
- Family relationship fields for family-care leave.
- Written cure notice listing specific missing or insufficient information when the employer says the certification is incomplete or insufficient.
- Designation notice or documented pending-designation status after review.

## Common Failure Modes

- "As needed" intermittent leave with no frequency, duration, episode pattern, treatment schedule, or flare-up estimate.
- Missing provider signature or contact information.
- Blank serious-health-condition, incapacity, treatment, or work-ability fields.
- Certification submitted after the due date with no documented good-faith effort or extension request.
- Employer or leave administrator says "incomplete" but does not identify the missing information in writing.
- HR wants to deny immediately without checking whether a cure opportunity was given.
- Employee wants an agent to contact HR or the provider, upload forms, or invent medical details.

## Guardrails

- Redact SSNs, full diagnosis narratives, full medical records, employee IDs, claim numbers, portal credentials, and unrelated personnel data.
- Label issues as `missing`, `unclear`, `deadline risk`, or `owner review needed`; avoid accusing fraud, abuse, bad faith, retaliation, discrimination, or unlawful denial.
- Escalate legal, medical, accommodation, discipline, retaliation, termination, and protected-activity questions to the authorized owner or qualified professional.
