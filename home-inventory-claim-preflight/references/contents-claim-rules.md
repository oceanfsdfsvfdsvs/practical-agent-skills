# Contents Claim Review Rules

Use these rules to keep the workflow factual, local, and conservative.

## Evidence Strength

- Strong ownership evidence: receipt, purchase-history export, dated photo/video showing the item in the home, serial/model record, appraisal, warranty record, or insurer-recognized prior inventory.
- Moderate ownership evidence: room walkthrough photo, family statement, gift record, bank/card transaction without item detail, repair record, or product manual tied to the household.
- Weak ownership evidence: memory-only item with no corroboration, generic description, missing room, missing category, or no replacement source.
- Damage/loss evidence should connect the item to the loss: damaged item photo, room-loss photo, fire/smoke/water context, theft report reference, restoration inventory, or total-loss context.

## Common Review Flags

- High-value items need stronger evidence than low-value consumables.
- Replacement cost should be supported by a receipt, quote, catalog page, model, retailer link, or defensible current equivalent.
- Age and condition matter because many claims start with actual cash value or depreciation before replacement-cost holdback is released.
- Jewelry, firearms, collectibles, art, cash, business equipment, tools, silverware, and specialty electronics may have policy sublimits or scheduled-property requirements.
- Home-office or business-use items should be separated for policy review instead of blended into ordinary household contents.
- Duplicate item rows, extreme quantities, and generic household goods should be grouped or clarified before adjuster review.
- Recovered, repairable, salvaged, or cleaned items should include consistent value notes.

## Guardrails

- Do not invent receipts, purchase dates, appraisals, replacement links, model numbers, photos, or ownership stories.
- Do not say an item is covered, excluded, underpaid, or unlawfully denied unless the user provides a written determination from the insurer, regulator, court, or counsel.
- Do not advise the user to sign a sworn proof-of-loss or settlement release. Prepare factual packet inputs only.
- Treat insurer forms as the source of truth when the user has them; map the inventory to those columns instead of forcing a different template.
