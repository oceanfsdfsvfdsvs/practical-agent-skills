# Workslop Patterns

Use this reference to spot AI-generated work that looks polished but pushes the real work onto the recipient.

## High-Risk Patterns

| Pattern | Signal | Recipient Cost | Repair |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unsupported certainty | "This will", "guaranteed", "best", or numeric claims without sources | Recipient must verify or absorb risk | Replace with cited facts, assumptions, or bounded confidence |
| Missing decision context | Draft gives updates but not the decision, ask, or owner | Recipient must infer what to do | Add the decision needed, owner, deadline, and next action |
| Generic synthesis | Broad advice that could apply to any company or project | Recipient learns nothing specific | Tie claims to the provided source material and constraints |
| Hidden assumptions | Dates, priorities, scope, budget, or dependencies appear without source | Recipient must unwind false premises | Label assumptions and ask for blockers only when needed |
| Responsibility fog | "We should", "the team", "soon", "follow up" | Work stalls because no one owns it | Assign owner, date, and observable output |
| Evidence laundering | Mentions sources but does not connect them to claims | Recipient must audit every sentence | Build a claim-to-source map or mark unverified claims |
| Risk silence | Customer, legal, security, financial, HR, or public risks are omitted | Draft may be unsafe to send | Add explicit risk notes and escalation owner |
| Over-rewrite | Repair changes commitments, numbers, or dates to sound better | Sender may create false promises | Preserve commitments unless source material supports the change |

## Claim Handling

- Keep supported facts.
- Mark unsupported facts as `unverified`.
- Convert recommendations into options when the evidence is incomplete.
- Remove filler that hides missing information.
- Escalate high-stakes claims instead of smoothing them into confident language.

## Minimum Repair Standard

A repaired artifact should answer:

1. Who is this for?
2. What decision or action should it enable?
3. What facts support the message?
4. What is assumed or missing?
5. Who owns the next step and by when?
6. What risk should not be hidden?
